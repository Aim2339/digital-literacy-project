# Cybercrime Case Study: UPI/Online Payment Fraud

## Overview

UPI fraud is one of the most common cybercrimes affecting students and young adults in India. In this type of fraud, attackers trick users into sharing sensitive information or approving fraudulent payment requests through digital payment platforms such as Google Pay, PhonePe, or Paytm.

## How the Fraud Happens

The attacker typically contacts the victim through SMS, WhatsApp, phone call, or social media. They may pretend to be a bank official, online seller, or customer support representative. The attacker then sends a fake payment request or QR code, claiming that the user will receive money after approving the request.

In many cases, the victim is told that they have won a prize, cashback, or scholarship. When the victim scans the QR code or approves the request, instead of receiving money, funds are deducted from their bank account. Sometimes, attackers also ask for OTPs or UPI PINs, which gives them full access to the victim's account.

## Who is Targeted

Students, young professionals, and people new to digital payments are commonly targeted. These individuals frequently use UPI apps and may not always verify payment requests carefully.

## Consequences

Victims may lose money instantly, and recovery is often difficult. In addition to financial loss, victims may experience stress and loss of trust in digital payment systems. Some users may also face identity theft if personal details are shared.

According to the National Cyber Crime Portal (cybercrime.gov.in), UPI fraud cases have increased significantly due to growing use of digital payments. Awareness and caution are essential to prevent such cybercrimes.
